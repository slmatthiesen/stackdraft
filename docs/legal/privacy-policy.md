This Privacy Policy explains how **S3 Ventures LLC** ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use **Drafture** (the "Service") at drafture.*. By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here.

The Service does not require an account and is intended for anonymous use. We aim to collect only what we need to operate, secure, and improve the Service.

---

## 1. Information We Collect

**a. Submitted prompt text.** When you use the Service, we collect and store the natural-language system descriptions and other prompts you submit ("Submissions"). Submissions may include whatever you choose to type — **please do not include secrets, credentials, personal data about others, or other sensitive information.**

**b. Generated designs.** We store the architecture designs, cost estimates, security baselines, and Terraform files the Service generates from your Submissions ("Generated Output").

**c. IP address.** We process the IP address associated with your requests for rate-limiting, enforcing per-IP daily caps, security, and abuse prevention.

**d. Basic request metadata.** We collect basic technical metadata associated with requests — such as timestamps, request/response sizes, token counts, coarse status information, and similar operational signals — to operate, monitor, secure, and improve the Service and to enforce token caps and our global spending ceiling.

We do not intentionally collect special categories of sensitive personal data, and we ask that you not submit them.

## 2. How We Use Information

We use the information described above to:

- **provide the Service** — generate designs, cost estimates, and Terraform from your Submissions and return them to you;
- **secure and protect the Service** — apply rate limits, per-IP daily caps, token caps, a global spending ceiling, bot detection, and other abuse-prevention measures;
- **improve the Service** — analyze usage and output quality, debug, and develop new features; and
- **curate the community gallery** — review selected Generated Output and, if approved, publish it publicly (see Section 4).

## 3. Automated Scrubbing of Submissions

Submissions pass through an automated scrubbing step intended to reduce the presence of certain obvious sensitive strings before storage or further processing. **This scrubbing is automated, best-effort, and imperfect; it is not guaranteed to detect or remove all sensitive information.** You remain responsible for what you submit — do not rely on scrubbing to protect secrets or sensitive data.
